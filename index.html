History of Body Piercing

The oldest fully preserved human discovered 5,300 years ago was found to have evidence of a pierced ear as well as 61 tattoos. Body modification has been around almost as long as recorded time. Most body piercing started for cultural or religious beliefs, and that still stands true today. For instance, the Maoris of New Zealand use tattooing as representation of different tribal roots. Earlobe piercing is considered a rite of passage in many Asian cultures. It is believed nostril piercing originated in the Middle East. In Mesoamerican civilizations, men were given giant plugs in their cheeks that were to be expanded gradually as they got older. Other piercings were marks of social standing. Some even believed piercings were actually helpful – some sailors during the medieval period said having a pierced ear could improve eyesight on the sea.

Modern-day mainstream piercing started taking off in the 1970’s. It is believed “hippies” returning from India brought the fashion back to America. It was then that body piercing studios really started to pop up in the United States. The rise of rock and roll, punk, and goth during this era is often credited for the rise of safe places to get tattooed and pierced.

Whether or not you have a specific reason to get pierced or you simply enjoy the way it looks, we’re happy to help you reach your goals! There are even piercings that are said to work like acupuncture. Any reason to get pierced is a valid reason to us.

Jewelry Available

We want body piercing to be accessible to everyone and we want each client to have a successful healing process. Our basic jewelry is all implant grade stainless steel. We do not use or sell any jewelry that contains nickel, a popular allergen. We have jewelry upgrades available for an upcharge. Upgradeable jewelry consists of titanium posts, 14k gold tops, etc. Some of our jewelry clients include Neometal, Diablo Organics, and BVLA.

Each piece will be sterilized with our hospital grade sterilization equipment. We use autoclaves to sterilize all equipment used and jewelry and maintain our autoclaves according to DHEC requirements.

Not only do we have a selection of jewelry to choose from for your initial piercing, we also have a showroom for other pieces if you’re looking for something new to put in an existing piercing. Our standard posts are flat backs, which are threadless studs that a gem screws onto at the top. This way you can keep the same posts and simply change out the tops as you like.

We can also special order items from any of our vendors if you want something special. BVLA is a popular vendor for customizable jewelry in which you can choose a different element for each part of the jewelry. Want rose gold instead of silver? They can do that. Want diamonds instead of opals? They can do that, too.

We also keep sterile saline solution in stock at the shop which is what we recommend using for cleaning your piercing during the aftercare process.
